Details

Creating a Parkinson's glove as a project for Techfest Nimbus was a challenging yet rewarding experience.

Parkinson's disease is a neurodegenerative disorder that affects movement, making it difficult for individuals to perform daily tasks. Our glove is designed to alleviate some of these difficulties by providing sensory feedback and support to the hand and wrist.

The glove is equipped with sensors that can detect tremors and other movements associated with Parkinson's disease. These sensors are connected to a microcontroller that processes the data and sends signals to motors embedded in the glove. The motors then vibrate in specific patterns, providing sensory feedback to the wearer and helping them control their movements.

Throughout the development process, we faced various challenges such as integrating the sensors and motors into the glove, and programming the microcontroller to produce the desired vibrations.
